About the Conference

Breaking Frames: Research Without Borders is a doctoral workshop in American & Cultural Studies designed for those who are tired of rigid frameworks that restrict what counts as “research.” We bring together PhD students and early-career researchers who want to experiment, cross boundaries, and be heard in a supportive environment.

At the heart of this event is a simple conviction: research is also process, risk, and conversation. Projects may sit within American Studies, Cultural Studies, English Literature—or move beyond any category. We value both critical clarity and formal experimentation, and we recognize that the personal stakes of research are integral to its intellectual force.

What you’ll find

  • Community: a space to share doubts, questions, and discoveries with peers navigating the same journey.
  • Constructive feedback: responses meant to strengthen your work, not box it in.
  • Visibility without rigid boxes: curiosity, creativity, and risk-taking are placed at the center.
  • Connections: build networks with other researchers who also want to push boundaries.

What “breaking frames” means

  • Opening up form: essays, fragments, experimental writing, or creative-critical blends (performance, film, theory, activism, literature…).
  • Asking questions about what academia ignores or silences.
  • Mixing methods and disciplines.
  • Sharing why your research matters to you—its situated, personal stakes.
  • Celebrating the unfinished, the speculative, and the disruptive as part of the research process.

Practical details

  • Date: September 24, 2026
  • Venue: Vitoria-Gasteiz
  • Format: Hybrid (in-person + online)
  • Fee: €60 (includes participation + coffee breaks)
  • Optional conference dinner: €20

Who should join

PhD students and early-career researchers in American & Cultural Studies, English Literature, and related fields—as well as anyone whose work resists or exceeds traditional academic categories.

Submission

We welcome proposals that cross boundaries of form, method, and discipline, and that highlight the personal and political stakes of research. Please submit an abstract (~250 words) and a short bio (up to 100 words) to emergingscholars2025@gmail.com.

Notification of acceptance: June 15, 2025.

Call for Papers

Doctoral Student Workshop in American & Cultural Studies

BREAKING FRAMES: RESEARCH WITHOUT BORDERS

We are tired of rigid academic frameworks that limit what counts as “research.” We believe PhD students and early-career researchers should have a space to speak freely, experiment, and be heard. This is YOUR CALL: an invitation to share the ideas, projects, doubts, and provocations that matter most to YOU. Whether your work sits within American Studies, Cultural Studies, English Literature—or wanders outside all categories—you are WELCOME here.

Why Join?

  • Community: Meet peers who are also navigating the PhD journey.
  • Feedback: Get responses from people who truly understand your situation.
  • Visibility: Present your project in an environment that values curiosity and risk-taking over rigid “academic boxes.”
  • Connections: Build networks with other researchers who want to push boundaries.

We Are Looking For Contributions That…

  • BREAK the rules of form (essays, fragments, experimental formats, creative-critical blends).
  • QUESTION what academia ignores or silences.
  • MIX disciplines, methods, or media (performance, film, theory, activism, literature…).
  • SHARE the personal stakes of your research—why it matters to you.
  • CELEBRATE the unfinished, the speculative, the disruptive.

Getting here

By air

  • Vitoria‑Gasteiz Airport (VIT): connections to the city centre/bus station by La Unión bus (~15 min; services coordinated with flight times).
  • Bilbao Airport (BIO): direct La Unión coach to Vitoria‑Gasteiz (~1h15–1h30; multiple daily departures). A new airport line connects Vitoria with Bilbao Airport.

By train

  • Vitoria‑Gasteiz station (Renfe Alvia/MD/Regional) is in the city centre; from there take the tram or a short taxi/bus ride to campus.

Within the city

  • Tram (Euskotren Tranbia): two lines; one terminus is Unibertsitatea, right by campus.
  • City buses: lines L8 (Circular–University), L9 (Gamarra–Zumaquera) and L3 (Betoño–Zumaquera) stop near the campus.
  • Tip: Vitoria‑Gasteiz is compact and flat—walking and cycling are easy options for most in‑city trips.

Parking

On‑street parking (“OTA”) is regulated; recent changes added longer visitor tariffs in parts of the city. Check local signage when you arrive.
